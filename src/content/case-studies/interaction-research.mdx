Research papers are full of useful connections, but they are difficult to explore one document at a time. In 2019, during my studies, I built an academic research explorer around a confidential dataset of Human-Computer Interaction papers.

I worked through the studies with traditional NLP techniques, then built a complete website that let people move between the wider dataset and individual papers. React and D3.js gave the interface its structure and visual language.

<ProjectImage src={coverImage} alt="Overview of the Human-Computer Interaction research explorer" caption="The explorer starts with a broad view, so a reader can decide where to look before opening a paper." />

Text-based research data does not naturally become a useful visual system. I cleaned it with Python and prepared JSON objects for D3, then designed exploration tools that could work at both dataset and paper level. The visualisation work was the part I enjoyed most, because the interface made the research genuinely fun to explore.

<ProjectImage src={paperImage} alt="Paper-level view in the research explorer" caption="A paper-level view keeps the detail connected to the patterns visible across the full collection." />

Because the dataset was confidential, I also implemented OAuth access control so the right people could use the explorer without exposing the underlying research.
